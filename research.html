Working papers

Coexistence of Centralized and Decentralized Markets

Online marketplaces that provide a venue to trade goods and services conveniently, such as Airbnb, Ebay, Etsy, and Uber, have seen a rapid growth in the last decade. When these marketplaces decide the rules of trade, their goal is to maximize their own profit; not the profit of the sellers or the welfare of the consumers. We employ a mechanism design approach to characterize the optimal rules of a profit-maximizing marketplace in an environment where agents arrive with endowments of a good. If the mechanism is the only trading venue, we characterize the optimal mechanism with an algorithm that ranks agents according to their virtual values and costs. Building on this characterization, we analyze the equilibria of a market participation game where agents choose between a mechanism and a decentralized market to understand the impact of their competition. Our main result finds that there are equilibria of this game, where agents with low and high values join the mechanism while the agents with intermediate values choose to join a decentralized market.
